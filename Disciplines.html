Disciplines

The Seven Islamic Sciences

Each discipline on Sual is treated with the depth it deserves — a serious introduction to the classical tradition, structured across Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced levels.

⚖️
Fiqh الفِقْه
Islamic Jurisprudence

Fiqh is the science of deriving practical rulings from the sources of Islamic law — covering worship, transactions, family law, and everyday conduct. On Sual, you'll work through core rulings on salah, zakah, sawm, and hajj at the beginner level, then move into the evidences and scholarly positions behind classical fiqh questions at intermediate and advanced levels.

Seerah السِّيرَة
Prophetic Biography

Seerah is the study of the life of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ — his character, his struggles, his companions, and the lessons drawn from his biography. Sual covers the major events of his life chronologically, from before prophethood through the Makkan and Madinan periods, with attention to the wisdom behind each episode.

Arabiyyah العَرَبِيَّة
Arabic Language

Arabiyyah is the gateway to every other Islamic science — the language in which the Quran, hadith, and the entire classical tradition were written. Sual's Arabiyyah content covers vocabulary, sentence structure, and reading comprehension, building toward independent engagement with classical Arabic texts.

Usul al-Fiqh أُصُولُ الفِقْه
Principles of Jurisprudence

Usul al-Fiqh is the methodology behind fiqh — how scholars derive rulings from the Quran, Sunnah, ijma, and qiyas. It covers the rules of interpretation, the categories of evidence, and the principles that govern juristic reasoning. This discipline is essential for understanding why scholars differ and how rulings are actually reached.

Sarf الصَّرْف
Arabic Morphology

Sarf is the study of word formation in Arabic — how a single root branches into dozens of related words through patterns of derivation. Understanding Sarf unlocks the internal logic of the Arabic lexicon, making it possible to recognize and derive new words rather than memorizing them in isolation.

Nahw النَّحْو
Arabic Syntax

Nahw is Arabic grammar — the rules governing how words relate to one another within a sentence, including case endings, sentence structure, and grammatical function. Mastery of Nahw is what allows a student to correctly parse a verse of the Quran or a line of classical prose and understand precisely what each word is doing.

Tafseer التَّفْسِير
Quranic Exegesis

Tafseer is the science of explaining the Quran — its meanings, context of revelation, linguistic nuances, and the rulings and lessons derived from each verse. Sual draws on classical tafseer works to present the meaning of selected verses alongside the scholarly reasoning behind each interpretation.
